# Sourcing — Channel & Country (WHERE)

Used in Phase 5, **only after the WHAT is settled.** Channel and country are a
separate decision about price vs risk vs logistics. Do not fold them into the
product choice.

> ⚠️ This is the one file whose facts decay — duty thresholds, VAT rules,
> platform reliability, and shipping norms change. **Verify any specific
> figure with live search before quoting it.** The *method* below is durable;
> the numbers are not. Never state a customs/VAT/threshold figure from this file
> or from memory.

## The decision is price vs risk vs logistics

Cheaper-elsewhere is rarely just cheaper. Weigh against it:

- **Landed cost**, not sticker price: item + shipping + import duty + VAT/sales
  tax + any platform/payment fees + (sometimes) a customs handling fee. A
  "half price" item can land at near-parity once these stack. Compute the full
  landed cost before comparing.
- **Warranty validity.** A warranty that doesn't honor cross-border or
  grey-import purchases is worth zero. For warranty-dependent goods this often
  decides it.
- **Return friction.** Cross-border returns can cost more than the item or be
  impossible. High-return-risk purchases (fit, compatibility uncertainty) tilt
  strongly toward local.
- **Counterfeit risk.** Higher on open marketplaces and for premium-brand goods;
  near-zero for genuine factory-direct commodity items.
- **Time.** A multi-week shipping gamble is fine for a tinkerer, ruinous for a
  deadline. Match to the person's actual time tolerance.

## China-vs-local is category-dependent (never a blanket rule)

The single most common mistake is a blanket "China is cheaper." It's wrong often
enough to be dangerous. Sort by where value concentrates:

- **Tilts toward China-direct** (e.g. 1688 / Taobao / JD / AliExpress): commodity
  accessories, cables, hobby and maker parts, generic components — things where
  warranty and after-sales contribute little value and the factory-direct price
  gap is large. The white-label reality (see value-curve.md) means you're often
  buying the identical product the local brand resells.
- **Tilts toward local** (e.g. UK retail): anything where warranty, safety
  certification, fast returns, or after-sales support dominate the value — and
  anything needed soon.

## Practical checks before recommending a channel

- Can the person actually transact there? (language, payment method, account,
  agent/forwarder needed for some China platforms.)
- Does the listing's price include or exclude the import charges that will hit
  on arrival?
- Is the seller's reputation independently verifiable, or only self-reported?

Privacy: never put the person's personal or payment details into URL parameters
or untrusted forms when researching or comparing channels.
