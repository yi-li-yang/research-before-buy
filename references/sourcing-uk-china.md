# Sourcing — Channel & Country (WHERE)

Used in Phase 5, **only after the WHAT is settled.** Channel and country are a
separate decision about price vs risk vs logistics. Do not fold them into the
product choice.

> ⚠️ This file's facts decay — duty thresholds, VAT rules, customs allowances,
> battery/air/sea rules, platform reliability. **Verify any specific figure with
> live search before quoting it.** The *method* below is durable; the numbers are
> not. Never state a customs/VAT/allowance figure from memory.

## Always price both sources

For any non-trivial purchase, pull **both the UK price and the China-domestic
price** so the comparison is real rather than assumed. The direction is not
fixed: imported Western/luxury brands are often *dearer* in China; domestic and
commodity goods and China-only products are often much cheaper. Don't guess which
way it goes — check.

## The three channels to weigh

For a UK-based buyer with China access, there are three concrete routes, with
different economics. Pick per item; often the answer depends on size, urgency,
warranty need, and whether a batch is already moving.

### 1. Buy local (UK) — immediate

- Full warranty and easy returns, no import friction, fastest.
- Highest price, usually.
- **Default when:** needed soon; warranty- or after-sales-dependent;
  region/firmware-locked; voltage-sensitive; heavy or fragile (shipping risk);
  or high return-risk (fit/compatibility uncertain).

### 2. Buy in China on a back-home trip (carry-back) — wait for the trip

- No shipping cost; you inspect before buying; good exchange on
  cheaper-in-China, light, region-neutral, or China-only goods.
- **Constraints:** trip date is a hard gate (needed before it → dead option);
  luggage weight/space; **region/firmware lock** (the big killer for connected
  devices — Chinese firmware, no Google services, app needs a Chinese number);
  voltage/plug (220V type A/I vs UK 230V type G — switching PSUs fine with an
  adapter, motor/heating appliances may not be); **China-only warranty**
  (effectively worthless in the UK); customs allowance on re-entry; no returns
  once home; spare lithium batteries/power banks must be carry-on and some are
  restricted.
- **Good for:** small, light, region-neutral, genuinely-cheaper-in-China or
  China-only items where warranty doesn't matter.

### 3. Buy in China + 海运 (sea freight, ~2 months) — slow, consolidated

- Cheapest per-kg for **bulky or heavy** goods; the route when air courier is
  uneconomical and you can wait.
- **海运 economics are per-shipment, not per-item.** A single small item by sea
  freight rarely justifies the wait and the fixed costs (forwarder, customs
  clearance, last-mile). The *same item added to a consolidation batch you're
  already shipping* is near-free marginal cost. So treat this as a
  **portfolio/batching decision**: flag the item as "a good candidate to add to
  your next sea-freight consolidation" rather than costing its shipping alone.
- **Constraints:** ~2-month transit (not urgent only); needs a forwarder/agent
  and usually a consolidation warehouse; customs/VAT on the *consolidated*
  shipment (verify current rules); damage risk over long transit; same
  region-lock/voltage/warranty issues as carry-back.
- **Good for:** heavy/bulky, non-urgent, region-neutral goods, especially when
  bundled with other China purchases.

## How to choose between the three

1. **Urgency first.** Needed soon → UK, full stop. The other two are for
   non-urgent buys.
2. **Region-lock / warranty / voltage.** Any of these binding → UK, regardless of
   price gap.
3. **Then size + batch state.** Small & light & a trip is coming → carry-back.
   Bulky/heavy, or a sea-freight batch is already being assembled → 海运
   consolidation. Otherwise re-check whether the China saving even survives once
   shipping and import charges are added (landed cost, below).

## Landed cost (applies to channels 2 and 3)

Compare *landed* cost, not sticker: item + (shipping or trip-attributable cost) +
import duty + VAT + forwarder/handling + any re-entry duty above the allowance. A
"half price" item can land near parity. Verify the current duty/VAT/allowance
figures live before relying on them.

## Practical checks (all channels)

- Can the person actually transact / carry / service it via this route?
- Does the quoted price include or exclude the charges that hit later?
- Is the seller's reputation independently verifiable, or only self-reported?

Privacy: never put the person's personal or payment details into URL parameters
or untrusted forms while researching or comparing channels.
